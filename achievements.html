Achievements

Throughout my life I've always loved competing in as many competitions as I could. They give me great drive and ambition and have kept me motivated throughout my life.


  • UKIEPC

    October 2019

    On the 19th of October 2019, I took part in the UKIEPC, my team of three came first in Ireland and 14th in the overall competition out of almost 200 university teams between UK and Ireland.

  • Irish Programming Team

    April 2017 – August 2017

    I was part of the Irish delegation for The International Olympiad in Informatics (IOI), which is an annual international informatics competition for high school students from various invited countries. After an intense three days of competitive programming battles, I was selected to represent Ireland at the prestigious 29th International Olympiad in Informatics that summer in Iran. IOI 2017 took part in Iran with 83 countries participating.

  • Irish Collegiate Programming Competition Secondary level 2nd Place

    March 2017

    My team "Stack Overflow Wizards" came second in the Irish Collegiate Programming Contest (IrlCPC) Secondary school level in UCC on the 25th of March 2017. Each team of competitors tackles a problem set that tests their problem solving, numeracy and computer programming skills. "This competition is not for novice programmers but those who have a passion for programming."

  • AIPO National Finals

    February 2017

    After qualifying for the finals through online qualification, I came 7th Overall in Ireland's senior category of the All-Ireland-Programming-Olympiad. This put me through to the next stage of the Team Ireland Selection Process.

  • Team Manager of F1 in schools National Finals 3 Awards

    November 2015 – April 2016

    During my transition year I created a Formula 1 in schools’ team. I recruited 5 other friends for the team. It was the first time our school ever took part in the competition, and we were the only non-technical school competing. We won 2 awards at the Regional's (Best Website – which was part of my role to create, Best Presentation) and at the National Finals we won 3 awards out of the 11 up for grabs out of the 74 teams that competed the awards we won were, Best presentation, Best Marketing and Best Newcomer.

  • Eircom Junior Spider Awards

    April 2015

    I was Honoured to be Shortlisted for the Eircom Junior Spider Awards with Arm Power Hosting. I was shortlisted for 'Best Individual Project' in the Giga Spiders Age group with my project 'Arm Power Hosting'. Since 1996 the eircom Spider Awards have honoured Irish individuals and organisations for their outstanding achievements online and celebrated the successes of the Irish internet and digital media.

  • Ireland's Best Young Entrepreneur Bootcamp

    September 2014

    In September 2014 Ireland's Best Young Entrepreneur Boot camp Cork 2014 took place in the Kingsley hotel Cork. I was selected to be one of 30 of Corks best young entrepreneurs to attend. We worked together over 3 days on our business, marketing and pitching skills in preparation for the county finals. I was the youngest participant selected in Ireland. With most participants in third-level education.

  • Cisco Transatlantic Challenge 2014

    January 2014

    In January 2014, I was selected as winner of the Cisco Transatlantic Coding Challenge at the ITLG Young Innovators event with my Project Taken World. Taken World was a browser based strategy game that I made using PHP and MySql.

  • Coolest Project Awards

    October 2013

    In October 2013, I came in 3rd place in games/web apps for Taken World at the Coderdojo Coolest Project Awards. "Aidan Molloy, who previously displayed at the Coolest Projects, was back again this year with “Taken World”, an online multiplayer strategy game, which he says has taken him a few hours a day for the best part of a year to develop."

  • Coolest Project Awards

    October 2012

    Coolest Projects was founded by Noel King and Ben Chapman in 2012. I was part of the first Coolest Projects Awards with my Project Cool Green Future which rewarded kids who recycled with prizes.
